Raclette is a traditional dish from Switzerland that involves melting cheese over potatoes, and it's a great way to use gruyere cheese. Here is a recipe for a vegetarian raclette with olives and onions:

Ingredients:

- 1 pound potatoes, sliced into thin rounds
- 1/2 cup gruyere cheese, grated
- 1/2 cup milk
- 1/2 cup flour
- 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 1/4 cup chopped onion
- 1/4 cup chopped black olives

Instructions:

1. In a large bowl, combine the potatoes, cheese, milk, flour, breadcrumbs, salt, and black pepper. Toss until the potatoes are evenly coated.
2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion and cook for 5 minutes, or until softened. Add the potato mixture and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the cheese is melted.
3. Stir in the olives and serve immediately.

Ingredients:

- 1 cup rolled oats
- 1/2 cup milk of choice
- 1/4 cup apple juice
- 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
- 1/4 cup chopped dried pineapple
- 1/4 cup maple syrup or honey (optional)

Instructions:

1. In a medium bowl, combine the oats, milk, apple juice, walnuts, pineapple, and maple syrup or honey, if using. Mix well to combine.
2. Cover and refrigerate overnight or for at least 2 hours.
3. Serve cold or at room temperature with additional milk or apple juice, if desired.

Note: You can adjust the proportions of the ingredients to taste and add other ingredients such as raisins, cinnamon, or coconut.
