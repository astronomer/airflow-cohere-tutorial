 Here is a recipe fortzue, a traditional Bhutanese dish that uses some of the ingredients you provided:

Ingredients:

- 2 cups chopped potatoes
- 1 cup chopped onions
- 1 cup chopped gruyere cheese
- 16 green olives, halved lengthwise
- 1 cup chopped fresh pineapple
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon dried basil
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 tablespoons lime juice
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro

Instructions:

1. In a large saucepan, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the potatoes and onions, and cook for about 10 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender. Drain and set aside.
2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the cooked potatoes and onions, and cook for about 5 minutes, or until the potatoes are golden brown.
3. Add the gruyere cheese, olives, pineapple, oregano, basil, chili powder, cumin, and black pepper, and cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the ingredients are well combined.
4. Remove from heat and stir in the lime juice and cilantro.

This recipe is traditionally from the Haa Valley region of Bhutan. It can be served with rice or roti for a complete meal.

As for a dessert suggestion, you could make a pineapple upside-down cake using fresh pineapple slices and a vegan cake batter. Simply arrange the pineapple slices in a baking dish, top with the cake batter, and bake according to the recipe instructions.
